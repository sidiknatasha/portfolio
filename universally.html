project universal.ly was started at the University of Washington to help research and iterated on solutions that can help streamline disability accommodation processes for student in higher education.

Process

As this project involves working directly with a client, the workflow is described through the diagram below.

Step 1: Clarify

For this re-design, it was important for me to clarify why it was being requested in the first place, and how the users themselves, who will be navigating the page and engaging with it, apart from the client (representing TechTogether), will also benefit from this. By approaching the project with this framework, the stakeholder's goals of getting more user engagement and sign-ups through the page can also be fulfilled.

Lack of context can be detrimental as it can cause miscommunication and/or mistakes. In this phase, it was crucial for me to try to get as much information I could from the stakeholder(s) and to communicate any questions or uncertainty I had about any of the prompts I was given. I also made sure to communicate my ideas surrounding accessibility ahead of time, before bringing it up again later through the designs I presented.

Step 2: Research

  1. Competitive analysis: identified other reliable hackathon event pages, and made comparison(s) on the types of informations they displayed, how they were displayed, etc. in a table. Took screenshots and annotated them as needed for references.
  2. Interviews (Qualitative): gathered user comments and feedback based on layout used previously. Synthesized user feedback into features/design improvements and scaled them within an Impact/Effort prioritization matrix.
  3. Secondary research (Quantitiative): gathered external data for validation.
  4. Identify issues/pain points: analyzed old design based on heuristic evaluation, competitive analysis, and user feedback from the interviews' Impact/Effort prioritization matrix. Annotated elements to work on specifically and why.

Key Takeaways

  • Its possible to advocate for inclusive design if that choice is articulated well.
  • Realistically, implementation may cause design changes to occur.
  • Choosing and conduting user research methods that fit time constraints (I only had a week to draft the first wifeframes!)

What I would Do Differently

  • In the future, if I were to work on a similar project, I would like to implement asking the stakeholders for access to their Google Analytics (or using Google Analytics)for more specific quantitative data to validate.
  • Request stakeholders to have more involvmement in implementation.

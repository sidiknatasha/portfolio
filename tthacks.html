TechTogether Event Page

ROLE(S): UX Designer

TOOLS(S): Figma, Google Drive, Slack, Zoom

DURATION: May - June 2021

TechTogether is the nation's largest initiative to address the gender inequities in the hackathon community, supporting over 10,000 hackers. As a UX Designer, I was tasked to re-design their event web page for the 2021 - 2022 season. The stakeholders were TechTogether's director and internal board of organizers, who wanted to increase user engagement through redesigning their event page. Communication mainly took place through Slack, with some Zoom calls during deadline days, where I presented progress and got feedback.

Problems

Clarify

For this re-design, it was important for me to clarify why it was being requested in the first place, so I spent some time interviewing the client, who was a stakeholder in this project. I also wanted to get an idea on how the target users themselves (site visitors) perceived the site. By approaching the project with this framework, I had a better idea on how a re-design can better fulfill my stakeholder's goal. Some design constraints were also set when it came to the use of colors and fonts as TechTogether had a previously established brand guideline they wanted me to follow appropriately.

Research

  • Competitive analysis: identified other reliable hackathon event pages, and made comparison(s) on the types of informations they displayed, how they were displayed, etc. in a table. Took screenshots and annotated them as needed for references.
  • Interviews (Qualitative): gathered user comments and feedback based on layout used previously. Synthesized user feedback into features/design improvements and scaled them within an Impact/Effort prioritization matrix.
  • Secondary research (Quantitiative): gathered external data for validation.
  • Identify issues/pain points: analyzed old design based on heuristic evaluation, competitive analysis, and user feedback from the interviews' Impact/Effort prioritization matrix. Annotated elements to work on specifically and why.

The Research phase made it clear that user engagement can be driven in the event page through a new page that was more accessible, mobile-friendly, and easy-to-read.

Impact

Through the implementation of these design changes, traffic increased by more than 2000 views daily via the organization's event page.

Key Takeaways

  • Its possible to advocate for inclusive design if that choice is articulated well.
  • Realistically, implementation may cause design changes to occur.
  • Conducting user research and using the appropriate methods within time constraints (I only had a week to draft the first wireframes).
  • How to communicate more effectively with stakeholders.

What I would Do Differently

  • Ideally would like more involvement in product implementation stages too, to ensure those better user experiences are translated to the final product outcome (dependent on stakeholders).
  • Due to pre-established time constraints, the research was quite "quick-and-dirty" with an overarching goal to get more context and validate pain points. With more time, I would've liked to spend more time conducting more in-depth research.

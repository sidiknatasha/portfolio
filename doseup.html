DoseUP

ROLE(S): UX Researcher, UX Designer

TOOLS(S): Adobe XD, Zoom, Discord

DURATION: March 26 - 28 2021

During the midst of the COVID-19 pandemic, vaccine limitations left many American's involuntarily unvaccinated or on waiting lists. DoseUP is a mobile application created to bridge those without access to the vaccine in the population and healthcare providers. This product was prototyped in a 3-day designathon with a partner, where it was awarded the Best Adobe XD Project.

Background

*At the time, COVID vaccines were still in the process of distribution in the States and eligibility guidelines only allowed a select few to gain access to the vaccine. On the other hand, healthcare providers would have extras by the end of a certain period that they'd also need to use - something people only encountered by luck or word-of-mouth at the right place and time. DoseUP was built to bridge the gap between these two parties (individuals wanting vaccine and healthcare providers) efficiently.

Key Takeaways

  • Its possible to advocate for inclusive design if that choice is articulated well.
  • Realistically, implementation may cause design changes to occur.
  • Conducting user research and using the appropriate methods within time constraints (I only had a week to draft the first wireframes).

What I would Do Differently

  • Ideally would like more involvement in product implementation stages too, to ensure those better user experiences are translated to the final product outcome (dependent on stakeholders).
